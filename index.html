clips/resumé

Hello! My name is Joe, and I'm an education
reporter based in Southern California.

I currently cover three public school districts (83 schools) and one community college in the Coachella Valley for The Desert Sun, a daily newspaper in Palm Springs, CA.

I take a watchdog and investigative approach to the beat. I file routine requests for public records, and I make an effort to present readers with clear and precise data.

But my first commitment is to cultivating relationships with sources. I'm a stickler for attending school board meetings, PTA meetings and high school football games.

After earning an M.S. in journalism from Columbia University in 2017, I worked at a magazine called Diverse: Issues in Higher Education covering equity issues until moving to The Desert Sun in 2018.

Before journalism, I was a PhD student in comparative literature at Rutgers University, specializing in theories of education during the Protestant Reformation. I left before finishing, and my parents never let it go.

Outside of work, I enjoy playing tennis, fermenting foods and beverages and camping. I also co-host a radio show at a local community college.
